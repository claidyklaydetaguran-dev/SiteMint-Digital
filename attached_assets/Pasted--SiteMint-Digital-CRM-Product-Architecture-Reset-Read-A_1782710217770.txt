# SiteMint Digital CRM — Product Architecture Reset

Read **ARCHITECTURE.md** and **DEVELOPMENT_RULES.md** first.

## IMPORTANT

From this point forward, stop thinking in individual UX phases.

Do **not** optimize for completing another page.

Do **not** optimize for adding isolated features.

Instead, build SiteMint Digital CRM like a commercial SaaS platform.

The objective is no longer:

> "Implement another component."

The objective is:

> "Ship complete business products."

Every implementation should leave one entire area of the CRM feeling finished, cohesive, and production-ready.

---

# PRODUCT ARCHITECT ROLE

For the remainder of development, act as:

* Senior Product Architect
* Senior Full Stack Engineer
* UX Architect
* Systems Designer

Before writing code:

* Audit the existing implementation.
* Identify duplicate systems.
* Identify overlapping routes.
* Identify duplicated business logic.
* Identify inconsistent UX.
* Consolidate whenever possible.
* Preserve all working functionality.
* Never rebuild something that already works.

The CRM should continuously become **smaller architecturally**, while becoming **more powerful functionally**.

---

# CORE PRODUCT PHILOSOPHY

Think in products, not pages.

Each product owns an entire business capability.

Each product has:

* its own navigation
* its own workflow
* its own data
* its own UI language
* its own lifecycle
* its own future roadmap

Do not scatter related functionality across unrelated pages.

Everything should have a clear home.

# NAVIGATION PRINCIPLES

Do NOT reduce navigation simply to reduce menu items.

Enterprise software succeeds because users always know where to go.

Prefer clear information architecture over artificially small menus.

Example:

SALES

* Contacts
* Sales Workspace
* Pipeline
* Deals
* Transactions
* Offers

is preferable to hiding everything under a single Workspace page.

Likewise:

Campaign Builder

Campaign Queue

Campaign Analytics

should remain separate destinations because they represent different workflows.

Likewise:

Reporting

Intelligence

Automation

should remain distinct products.

# DEVELOPMENT STRATEGY

From now on:

Never ask:

"What page should I build?"

Instead ask:

"What product am I completing?"

Each milestone should fully complete one business capability.

Before implementing:

1. Audit existing code.
2. Reuse existing components.
3. Remove duplicates.
4. Preserve existing functionality.
5. Improve UX consistency.
6. Expand capability.
7. Verify routing.
8. Verify responsiveness.
9. Verify type safety.
10. Regression test all related workflows.

---

# IMPLEMENTATION ORDER

Follow this roadmap unless dependencies require adjustment.

1. Communications Center
2. Sales OS
3. Marketing Hub
4. Delivery OS
5. Intelligence Center
6. Automation Center
7. AI Copilot

Each milestone should be treated as a complete product release.

Do not stop midway through a product unless blocked by an architectural dependency.

For every milestone, return:

1. Architecture audit
2. Duplicate systems removed
3. Files inspected
4. Files changed
5. New routes
6. Updated navigation
7. UX improvements
8. Functionality added
9. Regression testing performed
10. Remaining gaps

The objective is to build a cohesive SaaS platform—not a collection of disconnected pages.
